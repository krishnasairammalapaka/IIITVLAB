Mono-alphabetic Substitution Cipher References

Historical Background

The mono-alphabetic substitution cipher has been used for centuries to encrypt messages. Notable examples include:

  • Ancient Roman military communications
  • Medieval diplomatic correspondence
  • Early American Revolutionary War messages

Mathematical Foundation

The cipher operates on the principle of bijective mapping between two sets of alphabets.
