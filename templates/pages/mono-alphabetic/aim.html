Breaking the Mono-Alphabetic Substitution Cipher

A mono-alphabetic substitution cipher is a method of encryption where each letter in the plaintext is replaced by a different letter consistently throughout the message. This experiment explores the techniques to break such ciphers.

Key Components:

  • Substitution Key: A one-to-one mapping between plaintext and ciphertext alphabets
  • Encryption Process: Each letter is consistently replaced with its mapped counterpart
  • Decryption Process: Reverse mapping to recover the original text

Primary Objectives:

  • Understand the Cipher: Learn how mono-alphabetic substitution works and its characteristics
  • Master Cryptanalysis: Develop skills in frequency analysis and pattern recognition
  • Practice Decryption: Break encrypted messages using systematic approaches
  • Security Analysis: Evaluate the strengths and weaknesses of the cipher

Experiment Overview:

What You'll Learn

  • Frequency analysis techniques
  • Pattern recognition in text
  • Systematic decryption methods
  • Historical cryptography context

Skills You'll Develop

  • Analytical thinking
  • Problem-solving strategies
  • Cryptographic analysis
  • Statistical methods

Important Note:

While this cipher was historically significant, it is now considered cryptographically weak. However, understanding its principles and breaking methods provides valuable insights into cryptographic concepts and the evolution of encryption techniques.
